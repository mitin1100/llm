#### Money-Saving Tips & Alternative Ingredients:

To ensure you stay within or even below budget, consider these practical tips and ingredient alternatives:

1.  **Shop Smart for Produce:**
    *   **Sales and Seasonal Produce:** Always check for weekly sales and prioritize in-season produce. Farmers markets can also offer better prices and fresher ingredients.
    *   **Frozen Alternatives:** If fresh broccoli or mixed greens are expensive, consider using frozen broccoli florets or spinach. They are often cheaper and just as nutritious.
    *   **Root for Root Vegetables:** If sweet potatoes are pricey, regular potatoes or even carrots can be a good, inexpensive substitute, though they will alter the flavor profile slightly.

2.  **Pantry Power:**
    *   **Store Brands & Bulk Buying:** Opt for store-brand quinoa, chickpeas, and olive oil, which are typically more affordable than national brands. If you make quinoa often, buying a larger bag in bulk can significantly reduce the cost per serving.
    *   **Check Your Staples First:** Before heading to the store, check your pantry for existing olive oil, salt, pepper, and any optional garnishes like sesame seeds, cilantro, or parsley. This prevents unnecessary purchases.
    *   **Tahini Alternatives:** Tahini can be a pricier item. If you have a well-stocked pantry, you could try making your own (if you have sesame seeds) or, for a different flavor profile, a nut butter like almond or cashew butter thinned with lemon juice and water can be a substitute, though it won't be identical.

3.  **Protein Pointers:**
    *   **Tofu Deals:** Tofu is already a very cost-effective protein. Look for sales on larger blocks if you have space to store the leftovers for other meals.
    *   **Dried Chickpeas:** While canned chickpeas are convenient and affordable, buying dried chickpeas in bulk and cooking them yourself is even cheaper if you have the time for soaking and cooking.

4.  **Meal Planning:**
    *   **Utilize Leftovers:** This recipe serves 2. If you're cooking for one, you'll have a second meal ready to go, maximizing your ingredients' value.
    *   **Ingredient Overlap:** When planning other meals for the week, try to choose recipes that use similar ingredients (e.g., using the rest of the red onion, lemon, or mixed greens) to reduce waste and ensure you're getting the most out of your purchases.

By following these tips, you can enjoy a delicious, high-protein vegetarian Quinoa Buddha Bowl without breaking your $20 budget, and possibly even save a few dollars!
