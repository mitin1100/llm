### Healthy Improvement Suggestions (within a $20 budget)

The 'High-Protein Vegetarian Quinoa Buddha Bowl' is already rich in whole, nutritious ingredients. The primary area for improvement, especially if aiming for a slightly lower calorie count or a more balanced macronutrient distribution for everyday consumption, lies in adjusting portion sizes of the more calorie-dense components. These adjustments can be made without exceeding the $20 budget, as the current shopping list already meets the budget.

Here are some suggestions:

1.  **Adjust Quinoa Portion Size:**
    *   **Current:** 1 cup uncooked quinoa for 2 servings (yields approx. 3 cups cooked).
    *   **Suggestion:** Reduce to **2/3 cup uncooked quinoa** for 2 servings (yields approx. 2 cups cooked). This reduces carbohydrates and calories while still providing a good base.
        *   *Estimated impact (for 2 servings):* Reduces calories by ~209 kcal, protein by ~8g, carbs by ~36g, and fat by ~3.3g.

2.  **Moderate Dressing Ingredients (Tahini and Olive Oil):**
    *   **Current:** 3 tablespoons tahini and 1 tablespoon olive oil for the dressing.
    *   **Suggestion:** Use **2 tablespoons tahini** and **1/2 tablespoon olive oil**. The dressing will still be flavorful but less calorie-dense.
        *   *Estimated impact (for 2 servings):* Reduces calories by ~148 kcal, protein by ~2.6g, carbs by ~3.2g, and fat by ~15.6g.

3.  **Emphasize Non-Starchy Vegetables:**
    *   The recipe already includes good options like mixed greens, broccoli, red onion, and cherry tomatoes. Ensure generous portions of these lower-calorie, high-fiber vegetables to increase satiety without adding excessive calories. No cost increase here as these are already part of the budget.
